Book cover for "A Dama das Camélias" by Alexandre Dumas Filho.
Visual family: luminous impressionist oil painting with cinematic elegance.
Central scene: Marguerite Gautier in an opulent Paris salon, full figure leaning lightly against a chaise longue while holding a bouquet of white camellias at her waist; Armand Duval stands a few steps away in three-quarter view, visibly torn between desire and restraint; a gilt mirror, pale drapery and a tea table with a single crystal glass frame the emotional tension.
Color palette: ivory, pearl white, blush rose, champagne gold and soft emerald, with a small accent of deep carmine in the lips and camellia ribbon.
Lighting: bright late-afternoon interior light entering from tall windows, luminous and airy, high contrast but soft edges, elegant glow on skin and silk, no darkness swallowing the scene.
Framing: vertical composition for a book cover, medium-wide scene with both characters fully readable, Marguerite as the focal point in the foreground and Armand slightly behind, clear faces and gestures, nothing cropped awkwardly.
Character emotion: Marguerite is poised, seductive and fragile under the surface; Armand is transfixed, hopeful and already wounded. Their eyes must carry tension, longing and social danger.
Avoid: dark cover, night scene, solitary silhouette, generic shadowy man, sepia xilo look, teal-blue dominance, smoke, storm, muddy brown palette, cropped backs or shoulders only, expressionless faces, funereal wardrobe, overly prudish styling.
