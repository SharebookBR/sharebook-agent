Book cover for "A Escrava Isaura" by Bernardo Guimaraes, designed as a magnetic commercial literary cover with real visual seduction, not a timid classic reprint.

Visual family: staged realistic photography, cinematic period drama, elegant but high-impact.
Central scene: Isaura in full figure at the center of a luminous colonial veranda, wearing an ivory dress with refined texture, one hand gripping the iron railing and the other holding a crumpled letter near her waist; her face shows contained fear mixed with defiance. A few steps behind her, slightly out of focus but still legible, a powerful landowner in a dark green frock coat watches her with possessive tension. Beyond them, a sunlit Brazilian plantation landscape opens in haze and depth, suggesting beauty trapped inside violence.
Color palette: dominant ivory, warm gold, pale honey, soft jade green, and aged white, with a sharp carmine red accent in the letter seal and a floral detail to cut through the palette.
Lighting: bright late-afternoon light, warm and elegant, high contrast, glowing skin, luminous air, no murky shadows.
Framing: vertical composition, medium-long shot with Isaura occupying most of the cover, frontal and fully readable in thumbnail, veranda architecture framing her body and gaze, antagonist placed in the background for narrative tension.
Emotional direction: Isaura must feel radiant yet cornered, dignified, desirable, and emotionally alert; the man behind her must look predatory, entitled, and controlled rather than cartoonishly evil.
Texture and finish: premium historical drama poster, crisp facial expression, rich fabric detail, polished photographic realism.

Avoid: dark cover, night scene, lonely silhouette, smoky atmosphere, sepia xilogravura look, teal-and-orange blockbuster grading, generic sad woman staring away, cropped shoulder-only portrait, irrelevant back view, muddy brown palette, passive facial expression.
